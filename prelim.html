Prelim Term-End Reflection Outcome

Building the foundation

The prelim period introduced the expectations of the course and helped me establish my study habits, workflow, and confidence in completing academic requirements.

Reflection

During the prelim term, I learned the importance of building a strong foundation in database management in order to succeed in the rest of the course. The first few weeks introduced me to the basic concepts of databases, such as tables, rows, columns, and how data is organized in a structured way. I also learned how the subject would be handled, the kinds of outputs expected, and the discipline needed to complete weekly activities and exercises. At the beginning, I was still adjusting to the lessons, SQL syntax, and the step-by-step instructions for creating and managing databases. Although the adjustment was challenging, it helped me understand my strengths and the areas where I still needed improvement.

One of the most important things I learned during the prelim period was how to write simple SQL queries and understand the purpose of keys and relationships between tables. Written works tested my knowledge about database concepts, while laboratory activities required me to apply what I learned by creating tables, inserting records, and retrieving data using SELECT statements. I realized that understanding the lesson was only the first step because I also needed patience to practice coding, correct syntax errors, and review my queries before submitting them. There were times when I felt pressured by deadlines or confused by errors in my commands, but these experiences pushed me to become more resourceful, careful, and attentive to details.

Overall, the prelim term was a valuable starting point in learning databases. It gave me a clearer understanding of what the course requires and how I should respond as a student. The experiences during this period taught me to be more responsible, organized, and willing to ask questions whenever I did not understand a lesson. Even though there were challenges, they helped me develop better study habits that prepared me for the midterm and final parts of the course.
