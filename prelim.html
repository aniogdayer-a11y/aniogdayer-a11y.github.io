Prelim Term-End Reflection Outcome

Building the foundation

The prelim period introduced the expectations of the course and helped me establish my study habits, workflow, and confidence in completing academic requirements.

Reflection

During the prelim term, I learned the importance of building a strong foundation in order to succeed in the rest of the course. The first few weeks taught me how the subject would be organized, what kinds of outputs were expected, and how much discipline was needed to keep up with the lessons and weekly activities. At the beginning, I was still adjusting to the pacing of the class, the instructions for each task, and the different kinds of assessments we had to complete. Even though this adjustment was challenging, it helped me become more aware of my strengths and the areas where I still needed improvement.

One of the most important things I learned during the prelim period was how to follow instructions carefully and manage my time more effectively. Written works tested my understanding of the lessons, while performance tasks and activities required me to apply what I learned in a more practical way. I realized that understanding the concept was only the first step; I also had to be patient enough to practice, revise mistakes, and review my work before submitting it. There were times when I felt pressured by deadlines or confused by unfamiliar tasks, but these moments pushed me to become more resourceful and attentive.

Overall, the prelim term was a valuable starting point. It gave me a clearer idea of what this course demands and how I should respond as a student. The experiences I had during this period taught me to be more responsible, more organized, and more willing to ask questions whenever something was unclear. Although there were challenges, they helped me develop better habits that prepared me for the midterm and final parts of the course.
