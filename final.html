The final term gave me the chance to connect everything I learned, improve the quality of my work, and reflect on how much I developed throughout the course.

Reflection

The final term was the part of the database course where I could clearly see the results of my effort from the previous weeks. By this point, I had already developed a better workflow, stronger discipline, and a clearer understanding of how to approach database tasks and requirements. The lessons, activities, and outputs during the final period encouraged me to connect what I learned in the prelim and midterm terms, such as database design, SQL commands, and data relationships, instead of treating each topic separately. This helped me become more confident in applying concepts, solving problems, and completing work with greater accuracy and quality.

One of the biggest things I learned during the final term was the value of persistence and self-evaluation. Even when I already understood the flow of the course, I still encountered challenges such as limited time, overlapping deadlines, and tasks that required careful attention to detail. Some activities involved writing advanced SQL queries, creating reports, and managing multiple tables correctly. However, I was better prepared to handle these problems because I had learned how to organize my work, review commands carefully, and fix errors without giving up. Instead of feeling overwhelmed, I became more strategic in managing priorities and more mindful of how each activity contributed to my overall growth. I also appreciated how the course pushed me to be more independent while still reminding me of the importance of asking questions whenever something was unclear.

Looking back, the final term made me realize that this course was not only about completing deliverables but also about developing habits and attitudes that I can carry into future classes and real-life situations. It taught me to be patient, responsible, and committed to continuous improvement. This final reflection shows the knowledge, experience, and confidence I gained in learning databases, and that I am leaving the term with a deeper appreciation for the learning process.
